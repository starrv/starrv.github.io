My name is Valencia Starr and I am a software engineer with experience building and deploying web, mobile, and desktop applications using various technologies. Some of the technologies I use are Java, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, PHP, SQL,Python, Figma, Git/GitHub, Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud Platform, and Vercel.

About

I have a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from CUNY Lehman College and a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from Union College. As an undergraduate at Lehman College I worked as a full-stack developer intern. I also co-founded and became Vice President of the NSBE chapter at Lehman College.

I graduated from Lehman College with a 3.8 GPA with departmental honors in the Mathematics and Computer Science department. Immediately after graduation I completed an internship where I learned to build full-stack web applications using MongoDB, Express, AngularJS, and Node.js. I later worked at Revature as a Software Developer intern where I built an employment reimbursement system.

Over the years I continued to learn various technologies building and deploying numerous applications including a message board, a graphics editing software, a group chat application, a spit cost application, and a 3-dimensional adventure video game. I also deployed an Android application for startup company Change Food for Good to help farmers increase their crop production to reduce food insecurity.

These skills led to me teaching numerous courses in software development including becoming a Python Instructor at CUNY City College, a Technical Instructor at The Knowledge House, a Software Engineering Instructor at Flatiron School and a Adjunct Computer Science Lecturer at CUNY College of Staten Island. Outside of work I attend technical meetups, conferences, and hackathons. I continue to work on projects building applications, as this is my passion.
